Why this exists

Results are a terrible teacher

You need roughly a thousand bets to distinguish an excellent bettor from a break-even one, and that is at a confidence level a statistician would call weak. Three bets a day, that's eleven months. The entire time you are learning, you are paying the vig on every bet. The feedback loop is longer than the bankroll.

This is why advice like "stay disciplined" and "manage your bankroll" doesn't fix anything. It tells you how to lose slower. It doesn't tell you how to know whether you are any good. That question has an answer, it resolves in hours instead of years, and it is what this course is about.

The pledge

What this channel will never do

Not principles. Constraints. Each one is here because breaking it would make the education worse, and the education is the entire product.

01No picks. Not in a video, not in the description, not in a Discord, not as a bonus. A pick teaches you nothing and leaves you needing another one tomorrow. That's a subscription to someone's opinion, not an education.
02No win-rate claims we can't show you the arithmetic for. Every number in every episode gets worked on screen. If we paste in a finished graphic, we're asking for trust instead of demonstrating it.
03Our record is public and unedited. No login, no account, no paywall, at /record.html. It is wired straight to the database with no edit step, and it updates whether we like the direction or not. It's the only claim we make that can be independently checked, so it's the one that has to stay visible.
04The product stays off camera until episode 11. Showing a tool to people who can't yet evaluate it is how people get sold things. You should be able to judge it by the time you see it.
05We publish the arguments against us. Episode 13 attacks the foundation of this entire channel. When someone finds a real hole, it becomes an episode, not a deleted comment.

The part we'd cut if we were selling you something

Right now, EdgeDesk's own scanner does not beat the closing line. Its CLV is negative to flat. By the standard this entire course argues is the only one that matters, the tool has not proven itself.

We have a specific technical theory about why, and it's an episode later in the series. The number is on the record page, outside the paywall, and it updates on its own. If it turns green, you'll see it. If it stays red, you'll see that too.

Everyone else in this industry shows you a record after they already know how it ends. This is an experiment running in the open, and it might fail.
