What it scans for

Six kinds of mistakes the market makes.

Four scan live today; two more are on the way. Each one fires on the rules and internal math of the market, not a forecast. Severity reflects how defensible the fault is, not how big the number looks.

01
Dead Money

A team priced live on a board it cannot win: eligibility, transition rules, a postseason ban, or mathematical elimination from a pennant race. Phantom probability inflates the field; the live teams are underpriced.

02
Logical Inconsistency

Nested markets must obey order: win ≤ qualify, win-title ≤ make-playoff. When a book inverts that across two screens, it's not a disagreement. It's impossible.

03
Stale Futures

An obscure board that hasn't moved after a real trigger: a transfer, an injury, a coaching change. Staleness against a named event is a window before the reprice.

04
Overround Anomaly

A board whose total vig sits well outside its peer band: thin, loosely-priced markets the book hasn't tightened. A screening signal, not a standalone bet.

05
Dead-Heat / Top-N soon

Top-N markets where the number of qualifiers and dead-heat rules make a posted price structurally off the true settlement math.

06
Structural Asymmetry soon

Scheduling, rest, tiebreaker and bid-thief spots the book is slow to price, heaviest in college conference tournaments.
