Every other tool runs model vs. line — its number against the book's. Faultline runs structure vs. line and market vs. itself. It flags errors that come from the rules and internal math of the market, not from a forecast — the mispricings a better model can't find and a competitor can't copy without knowing the sport. Each fault ships with a receipt: the structural reason, verifiable by you.

▲ DEMO BOARD — illustrative sample data. Production binds to the live Odds API feed + your maintained eligibility/rules table via the existing capture pipeline.

Faultline is a quantitative research tool. It surfaces probability and structural classifications derived from public market data. It is not betting advice, not a recommendation to wager, and not a guarantee of any result. Flagged faults are estimates and structure-detection is not a prediction of outcomes.
